Sciatica / Radiating Leg Pain – Natural TCM Treatment

What is Sciatica?

Sciatica occurs when the sciatic nerve is irritated or compressed. The pain typically radiates from the lower back through the buttock down the leg.

Typical Symptoms

  • • Sharp or pulling pain from the back into the leg
  • • Pain in the buttock or back of the thigh
  • • Numbness, tingling or burning sensations
  • • Worsening while sitting or bending forward
  • • Relief when lying down or walking

TCM Perspective on Sciatica

In Traditional Chinese Medicine, sciatica is often caused by meridian blockage, invasion of cold or dampness, or weakness of the Kidney or Liver energy.

Common TCM Patterns

  • • Qi and Blood stagnation along the sciatic pathway
  • • Cold or damp obstruction in the lower back
  • • Kidney Qi deficiency (chronic pain)
  • • Liver Wind / muscle tension
  • • Blocked meridians due to poor posture

How TCM Helps with Sciatica

TCM addresses both the pain and its underlying cause. With acupuncture, tuina and circulation-enhancing techniques, significant and lasting improvements can often be achieved.

Treatment Methods

  • • Acupuncture: releases blockages along the nerve
  • • Tuina Massage: relaxes muscles, improves mobility
  • • Cupping: enhances blood circulation & metabolism
  • • Moxibustion: warms meridians, ideal for cold-induced pain
  • • Herbal Therapy: strengthens Kidney & Liver, reduces inflammation

When Should You Seek Medical Attention?

Sciatica is often harmless, but certain warning signs (“red flags”) must be medically evaluated.

  • • Severe pain after an accident or fall
  • • Numbness or loss of strength in the leg
  • • Problems with urination or bowel control
  • • Fever or unexplained weight loss
  • • No improvement despite treatment
